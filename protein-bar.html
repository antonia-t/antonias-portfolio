The protein bar market
has a flavour problem.

Walk into any gym, supermarket, or health food store and the protein bar shelf looks identical — sweet, chocolate-coated, dessert-adjacent. The implicit assumption of the entire category is that protein consumption has to taste like a treat.

But a significant consumer segment — athletes, people who eat clean, anyone who's ever found a sweet bar nauseating mid-workout — wants something different. A protein bar that tastes like food, not a reward.

Core insight
"The protein bar market is built on the assumption that health has to be disguised as dessert. We think that's wrong."

how we're building it

Validation before
everything else.

The Entrepreneurship Avenue program at WU Vienna provides a structured framework for taking an idea from problem exploration to investor-ready pitch. We're using it properly — starting with the problem, not the solution.

1
Problem definition
Mapped the existing protein bar landscape. Identified the sweet-only assumption as a genuine gap, not just a personal preference. Validated through conversations with target consumers — athletes, gym-goers, health-conscious professionals.
2
Solution exploration
Developed initial product concepts around savory flavor profiles — Mediterranean herbs, smoked, umami-forward. Tested concept appeal through structured feedback sessions.
3
Iterative feedback
Each iteration refined by real user responses. What people said they wanted vs what they actually responded to. Currently in this phase — refining the concept based on feedback cycles.
4
Pitch preparation
Building toward the Entrepreneurship Avenue finals — a structured pitch to an external jury. Coming up.
"The most useful thing the program taught me: fall in love with the problem, not the solution."
This is the project I'm most uncertain about — and that's the point. Maison Éternelle was a competition brief. BCS was a live job. This one is genuinely mine, built from scratch, with no guaranteed outcome. That uncertainty is the most interesting part of working on it. Watch this space.
