the problem
why
now? →

Every luxury scent
can be copied.

Luxury fragrance is the fastest-growing beauty category — and its most vulnerable one. The same fragrances driving this growth — Libre by YSL, Paradoxe by Prada — are the most duped ones. 60 million TikTok views on #dupeparfume. 28% of global luxury sales impacted. A €52B market with a credibility problem.

"The dupe isn't just a cheaper product. It's a direct challenge to the idea that luxury is worth paying for."

The brief was clear: how does YSL Beauty protect and grow its fragrance business in a world where any scent can be reverse-engineered and sold for €15 on TikTok Shop?

fix ↓
the concept

Your scent. Your world.
Your rebel act.

Maison Éternelle is a personalized, refillable, evolving fragrance platform for YSL Beauty. The insight: the only fragrance that can't be duped is one that's uniquely and continuously yours. Personalization isn't just a feature — it's the anti-dupe mechanism.

1
Explore your world
Three immersive environments — Ocean, Rainforest, or Urban — each with its own scent and eco-project partner. Accessible in flagship stores via AR or anywhere via WebAR.
2
Build your Scent DNA
A questionnaire maps emotions, memories, and preferences to a unique profile. No two profiles are identical.
3
Receive your fragrance
Base note extracted from your world's real waste via L'Oréal's Osmobloom™ technology. Accord constructed on your Scent DNA. NFC chip embedded in the bottle activates your Scent Passport instantly.
4
Live with it
At every refill, your formula slightly evolves with season and history. The only fragrance that grows with its owner.
the strategy
Sustainable
−44% glass, −67% plastic, −100% metal vs repurchase. Osmobloom™ uses real waste as raw material. 5% net revenue per sphere to its NGO partner.
Scalable
Zero greenfield build — 100% existing L'Oréal infrastructure. One shared AI + NFC engine powers every brand and market. New brand = new world, same architecture.
Inclusive
Gender-neutral by design. WebAR means any customer anywhere can explore their world — no flagship required. Personalisation through a simple questionnaire.

honest reflection
what i'd
change →
If I were to do this again — I'd define the target consumer more explicitly. Who exactly is the Maison Éternelle customer? Age, mindset, relationship with luxury, TikTok behavior. We implied it throughout but never stated it directly. I'd also address the competitive landscape more head-on — how does this sit against Initio or Frédéric Malle, who already play in ultra-personalized niche fragrance territory? The concept is strong. The edges could be sharper.
